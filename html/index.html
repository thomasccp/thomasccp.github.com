Highlights

  • 10+ years of FPGA experience.
  • PhD from Imperial College London, with full scholarship.
  • Authors of 27 publications in peer-reviewed international journals and conferences.

I obtained my PhD in Computing at Imperial College London in 2014, under the supervision of Professor Wayne Luk. Before that, I received my BEng and MPhil degrees in Computer Science and Engineering from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, in 2008 and 2010, respectively.

I am currently an electronic design engineer in Altera European Technology Centre (now part of Intel). My responsibility involves developing embedded hardware and software for system-on-chip FPGA devices, for instance phasor measurement unit, motor control applications, battery management system for hybrid electric vehicles, and functional safety of programmable electronics. I worked as a placement student in Research and Development department of ARM Ltd, Cambridge in 2010. I also managed the heterogeneous computer cluster of the Custom Computing Research Group.

My research includes reconfigurable computing for high performance and real-time applications, particularly those involving Sequential Monte Carlo method (SMC) and Proximity Query process (PQ). Example applications include air traffic management, mobile robot localisation, human-robot collaborative control for surgery, and financial modelling. See my publications, DBLP list, Google Scholar Citations and CV (A4, Letter) for details.

 

Specialities

  • High performance computing using FPGAs, GPUs and multi-core CPUs.
  • Embedded real-time control applications in industrial, automotive and energy industries.
  • Functional safety of programmable electronics (IEC 61508 and ISO 26262).

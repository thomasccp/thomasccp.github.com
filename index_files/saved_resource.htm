Biography

I received my B.Eng. and M.Phil. degrees from Computer Science and Engineering Department at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, in 2008 and 2010, respectively. With the support of the Croucher Foundation Scholarship, I am pursuing my Ph.D. degree in Department of Computing at Imperial College London under the supervision of Professor Wayne Luk.

I am currently an electronics engineer in Altera European Technology Centre. My responsibility involves developing embedded hardware and software for system-on-chip FPGA devices, and designing AMP/SMP software solutions. I worked as a placement student in Research and Development department of ARM Ltd, Cambridge in 2010. I also managed the heterogeneous computer cluster of the custom computing research group.

My research includes reconfigurable computing for high performance and real-time applications, particularly those involving Sequential Monte Carlo method (SMC) and Proximity Query process (PQ). Example applications include air traffic management, mobile robot localisation, human-robot collaborative control for surgery, and financial modelling. See my publications, DBLP list, Google Scholar Citations and CV (A4, Letter) for details.

 

Research Interests

  • Optimising reconfigurable devices for real-time applications
  • Improving design efficiency of such devices for high-performance computing
